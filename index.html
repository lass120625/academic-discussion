My Rationale & Deliberations

My hesitation is not without reason; it stems from a comprehensive consideration of my career goals, observations of industry trends, and my personal circumstances. The following points are the key factors I have been weighing.

💡 Weighing Breadth vs. Depth

I am weighing the broad technical foundation of the MIT program against the specialized, hands-on learning in DECO courses, which aligns more closely with my UI/UX and future PM goals. Both are valuable, but I need to determine which is more critical for me.

🤖 Contemplating Future Value

Considering the rise of AI, I wonder if specializing in human-centered skills (like design thinking and empathy) is a more resilient long-term career investment. This is what I see as the core advantage of the MIDes program.

⚖️ Balancing Study and Reality

As a single mother, my study time is very limited, which forces me to be pragmatic. I'm concerned that the extensive theoretical learning in technical courses may offer less immediate, practical benefit for employment compared to more applied design skills.

Discussion Points & Advice Sought

Based on the above, I still have many questions and uncertainties. I would be very grateful to hear your professional opinion to help me make the most suitable decision. The main points I wish to discuss are:

1. Career Path Planning (UI/UX -> PM)

I am interested in a career path from UI/UX Designer to Product Manager (PM). Could you advise on the advantages and challenges each master's degree would present for this long-term goal? Is the user-centric mindset from ID or the technical feasibility assessment from IT a more critical starting point?

2. Maximizing Learning Efficiency

Given my limited time, I want to maximize the benefit of every course. If I stay in IT, how would you suggest I build my design skills? If I switch to ID, what are the most critical technical skills I should focus on learning outside of class?

3. Leveraging My Unique Background

My undergraduate degree in journalism has given me a sensitivity to content creation and storytelling. Do you think this could be a unique advantage in the UI/UX or PM fields? How can I best combine this experience with my current studies to create a distinctive profile?

4. Questions on Job Market Realities

I have heard that some local UI/UX work in Australia is outsourced, which is a concern for me. Additionally, outside of corporate roles, are there potential career paths for Interaction Design in the public sector or academia? I would like a more pragmatic understanding of the overall employment landscape.

Thank you for your time and I look forward to our conversation.
